## Cross-Company Observations

### Observation 1: Founder LinkedIn Activity is the Single Best Predictor of Pipeline Quality

Across all 25 companies, the most reliable predictor of outreach success potential was whether the founder actively posts on LinkedIn. Companies where founders publish 2–3x per week (Masai, Leverage Edu, Jar App) offer significantly warmer cold outreach opportunities than companies with inactive founders (Zaggle, BetterPlace).

**Implication for DT:** Build a LinkedIn monitoring cadence — follow all 25 founders and engage with their content before sending any DM.

### Observation 2: EdTech Companies Don't Need Convincing That Learning Quality Matters

The biggest sales friction for DT's LMS is convincing buyers that structured, methodical learning is worth paying for. EdTech founders already believe this — it's literally their product. This removes the most common sales objection. Non-EdTech targets (FinTech, HRTech) require a longer explanation of why structured learning matters to their teams.

### Observation 3: The Three Existing Clients Need Account Success Attention

Growpital, Kringle.ai, and IPx are already paying DT clients (at some level). That score profile mainly reflects where they are in their growth stage, not how important the relationship is. DT should invest in making these early clients successful enough to become case studies, which then become the best possible asset for acquiring the HOT tier targets.
