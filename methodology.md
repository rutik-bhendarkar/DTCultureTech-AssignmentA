# Methodology
## DT CultureTech — Target Company Research Process

---

> **Document Purpose:** This file explains exactly how the 25 target companies were identified, filtered, scored, and prioritized. Every decision is documented with reasoning so the research can be replicated or audited by another analyst.

---

## 1. Research Question

**Central Question:** Which 25 Indian product startups are the best candidates for DT CultureTech's B2B outreach for its SD LMS platform, and in what priority order?

**Why This Matters:**  
DT CultureTech needs to build a qualified sales pipeline. Reaching out to 25 wrong companies wastes time. Reaching out to 25 right companies with specific, personalized messaging creates real pipeline. This methodology is designed to make every outreach message feel like it was written for that specific company — not sent to a generic list.

---

## 2. Understanding DT CultureTech's ICP First

Before identifying any target companies, I spent time understanding WHO DT CultureTech actually is and WHAT kind of company would buy from them.

**Sources used to understand DT's ICP:**
- DT CultureTech's Internshala job listings (primary source for framework names and client descriptions)
- DT's MCA registration details (CIN: U70200TG2020PTC139812 — verifies Hyderabad HQ, founding date March 2020)
- Tracxn profile for DT CultureTech (verifies funding, investors, directors)
- DT's listed early clients on professional platforms (Growpital, Kringle.ai, IPx)

**What I concluded DT's ICP looks like:**

| ICP Parameter | Criteria |
|---|---|
| Company Stage | Seed to Series D (can afford a platform; not pre-revenue, not enterprise) |
| Team Size | 20–500 employees (enough scale to justify a learning platform) |
| Industry | EdTech, FinTech, HRTech, SaaS — sectors where product innovation culture is valued |
| Leadership Type | Founder-led or innovation-conscious CTO/CPO in leadership |
| Location | Pan-India (DT is remote-capable; Hyderabad gives proximity advantage) |
| Decision Maker | Founder, Co-Founder, CPO, CTO — not HR procurement (at seed/early stage) |
| Budget Signal | At least Seed funding completed, or bootstrapped with ₹10Cr+ revenue |

**What DT does NOT sell to:**
- Large enterprises (procurement cycles too long for a seed-stage company)
- Pre-product startups (no team to train yet)
- Non-tech companies without product culture
- Government institutions (not the current business model)

## 3. Company Identification Process

### Step 1 — Define the Search Universe

I used the following categories to generate a candidate list of 60+ companies, which was then filtered down to 25:

**Primary Sources:**
- Internshala's company database (filtered by: Hyderabad, Bengaluru, startup, product, funded)
- Tracxn's Indian startup database (sector filters: EdTech SaaS, FinTech, HRTech)
- Inc42's Indian startup funding tracker (series-tagged companies 2021–2024)
- LinkedIn Company Search (filtered by: India, 11–500 employees, "startup" keyword)
- YourStory's funded startup lists (annual funding roundup articles)

**Secondary Sources:**
- T2D3 (early-stage Indian SaaS database)
- SaaS Insider India community lists
- DT's own public profile (for existing client names — direct ICP evidence)

### Step 2 — Apply Hard Filters (Eliminate)

Companies were eliminated from the long-list if they met ANY of these conditions:

| Filter | Reason for Exclusion |
|---|---|
| >1000 employees | Procurement cycles too complex for DT; wrong buyer psychology |
| Pre-seed / Pre-revenue | No budget for a B2B platform |
| PE-owned or large corporate subsidiary | Decision-maker is not the founder; innovation culture likely commoditized |
| Pure services company (consulting, BPO) | No product team to train; DT's LMS requires product orientation |
| Government or public sector | Outside DT's current go-to-market |
| Non-Indian HQ | DT's regulatory and relationship context is India-specific |
| Company dissolved or inactive (MCA check) | Not reachable; research wasted |

### Step 3 — Apply Soft Filters (Prioritize)

After hard filtering (~40 companies remaining), soft filters were used to rank and select the final 25:

| Positive Signal | Reason |
|---|---|
| Founder active on LinkedIn (posts 2+ times/month) | High reachability; pitch has lower friction |
| EdTech or learning-adjacent sector | Highest domain alignment with DT's product |
| Recent funding news (2022–2025) | Budget signals; company is in growth mode |
| Hyderabad-based | DT's home city — relationship advantage |
| Named in DT's own client/partner mentions | Highest confidence ICP fit |
| Active hiring in product/tech roles | Company is growing → team development need |

### Step 4 — Include Existing DT Clients Deliberately

Three confirmed DT clients (Growpital, Kringle.ai, IPx) were included in the 25 despite lower scores than top EdTech targets. Reason: A Business Analytics intern should account for existing relationships in the pipeline — they represent account expansion opportunities, not just new acquisition.

## 4. Scoring Methodology

### Scoring Architecture

Each company is scored on 6 criteria, each rated 1–5. Maximum score = 30.

**Scoring is NOT subjective.** Each score maps to specific, observable evidence.

### Criterion-by-Criterion Scoring Rules

---

**C1 — Innovation Culture Readiness (1–5)**

*What this measures:* Does the company's team actually invest in learning, research, and problem-solving? DT's LMS only works if the company values structured thinking. A company with no learning culture is a hard sell.

| Score | Evidence Required |
|---|---|
| 1 | No public evidence of team development, training, or learning culture |
| 2 | Basic onboarding mentions; no structured L&D |
| 3 | LinkedIn posts about team learning; blog posts on product thinking |
| 4 | Dedicated product/engineering blog; public talks by team members; hackathon participation |
| 5 | Documented innovation culture (research publications, thought leadership, DT-adjacent methodology) |

---

**C2 — Team Size Fit (1–5)**

*What this measures:* Is the team large enough to justify a platform purchase, but small enough that DT's intern-driven model works?

| Score | Evidence Required |
|---|---|
| 1 | <10 employees (too small; no budget or need) |
| 2 | 11–30 employees (borderline; budget tight) |
| 3 | 31–100 employees (moderate fit) |
| 4 | 101–300 employees (good fit; DT's sweet spot) |
| 5 | 301–500 employees (optimal; clear platform need) |

*Note: Above 500 is not automatically 5/5 — it reduces accessibility and increases procurement friction.*

---

**C3 — Decision Maker LinkedIn Access (1–5)**

*What this measures:* Can DT's BD team actually reach the decision-maker via LinkedIn? A great ICP fit with an unreachable founder is a dead-end.

| Score | Evidence Required |
|---|---|
| 1 | No LinkedIn profile found |
| 2 | Profile exists but last active >6 months ago |
| 3 | Active profile, occasional posts, small network |
| 4 | Regular poster (1–3x/week), 5K–20K followers |
| 5 | Thought leader on LinkedIn (20K+ followers, daily posts, high engagement) |

*Source: LinkedIn profiles manually checked during research week.*

---

**C4 — Budget Readiness (1–5)**

*What this measures:* Does the company have financial capacity to pay for DT's platform?

| Score | Evidence Required |
|---|---|
| 1 | No funding; revenue <₹1Cr; bootstrap at very early stage |
| 2 | Small seed funding (<$1M); limited runway |
| 3 | Seed funding ($1M–$5M) or bootstrap ₹5–20Cr revenue |
| 4 | Series A ($5M–$20M) or ₹20–100Cr revenue |
| 5 | Series B+ (>$20M) or profitable at scale |

*Note: Revenue figures are estimated from funding databases (Tracxn, Entrackr) and should be treated as indicative. See verification_process.md.*

---

**C5 — Personalization Hook Strength (1–5)**

*What this measures:* Is there a recent, specific, unique trigger that makes a cold LinkedIn message feel warm?

| Score | Evidence Required |
|---|---|
| 1 | No recent news; generic company with no distinct hook |
| 2 | Old news (>2 years) or very generic (e.g. "congratulations on your launch") |
| 3 | Funding announcement; team expansion news |
| 4 | Specific product launch; specific LinkedIn post by the DM that can be referenced |
| 5 | DM recently published about a problem DT directly solves; OR existing DT client (highest personalization possible) |

---

**C6 — Domain Alignment (1–5)**

*What this measures:* How naturally does DT's SD LMS pitch land in this industry?

| Score | Evidence Required |
|---|---|
| 1 | Completely unrelated (e.g. manufacturing, logistics) |
| 2 | Distant relevance (e.g. consumer eCommerce) |
| 3 | Adjacent (FinTech, HealthTech — product teams exist but learning culture pitch requires more work) |
| 4 | Strong adjacency (HRTech, PropTech — L&D is part of their product space) |
| 5 | Direct alignment (EdTech, Bootcamp, Upskilling — DT's methodology is the product these companies understand) |

---

### Verdict Calculation

```
Total Score = C1 + C2 + C3 + C4 + C5 + C6

HOT  = Total Score ≥ 22  (immediate outreach priority)
WARM = Total Score 14–21 (secondary pipeline)
COLD = Total Score ≤ 13  (removed from list)
```

*No COLD companies appear in the final 25 — all low-scoring candidates were eliminated during Step 2 hard filtering.*

## 5. Revenue Estimation Logic

Most Indian startups are private companies with no audited revenue publicly available. Where revenue was not publicly confirmed, the following estimation logic was used:

| Funding Stage | Typical Revenue Range (Indian Startups) | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-seed | <₹1 Cr | Generally pre-revenue |
| Seed ($500K–$2M) | ₹1–10 Cr | Early traction stage |
| Series A ($3M–$10M) | ₹10–50 Cr | Growth stage |
| Series B ($15M–$50M) | ₹50–200 Cr | Scale stage |
| Series C/D ($50M+) | ₹200–1000 Cr | Mature growth |
| Bootstrapped (profitable) | Estimated from employee count and industry benchmarks |

**All revenue estimates are marked as "Revenue Band" (a range), never as exact figures, to reflect the uncertainty of these estimates.**

*Primary revenue reference: Entrackr (for companies that have published data), Tofler/MCA (for audited filings where available), and Tracxn revenue estimates.*

## 6. LinkedIn Verification Process

For each Decision Maker, the following LinkedIn check was performed:

1. Search for the person's name + company name on LinkedIn
2. Confirm their current role matches what public sources describe
3. Check "Activity" section — are they posting? When was the last post?
4. Note follower count (visible on public profiles)
5. Check if mutual connections exist (DT's network advantage)
6. Assign LinkedIn Presence Score (1–5) based on the rubric above

*LinkedIn data accessed during research. Profiles are public unless otherwise noted. Some founders have private profiles — these are scored lower (2/5 max).*

## 7. Rejection Criteria (Why Some Companies Were NOT Included)

Companies were rejected from the final 25 for these documented reasons:

| Rejection Reason | Example of Applied Filter |
|---|---|
| Too large (enterprise scale) | Razorpay, Meesho, Swiggy — procurement cycle misaligned |
| Pre-product / pre-revenue | Rejected 3 early-stage startups with no team yet |
| Pure services (no product) | Consulting companies without a product team |
| LinkedIn-dead leadership | 2 companies where no founder/DM had reachable LinkedIn |
| Acquired or PE-controlled | 1 company that was acquired in 2023 — decision maker no longer accessible |
| Sector mismatch | 4 manufacturing/logistics companies — no product innovation culture alignment |
| Revenue too high + enterprise procurement | 2 unicorns where DT's LMS would be a procurement item, not a founder decision |

---

## 8. Prioritization Logic for Final 25

Once scoring was complete, the final 25 were prioritized as follows:

1. **Highest score first** — companies scoring 24–27 form the immediate outreach priority
2. **Within same score — EdTech companies get priority** — highest domain alignment
3. **Within same score + sector — LinkedIn-active founders get priority** — highest conversion probability
4. **Existing DT clients included regardless of score** — account expansion is a separate strategic objective
5. **Geographic diversity maintained** — not all Bengaluru; includes Hyderabad, Noida, Mumbai, Gurugram

---

*This methodology was applied consistently across all 25 companies. Deviations are noted in `company_research_notes.md`.*

## 7. Why Some Broader Companies Were Included

The list is not restricted to one narrow company type. A few product-led or platform-led firms were kept on purpose because they still show the signals DT cares about: founder involvement, active product teams, visible growth, and a team that may benefit from structured internal learning. In other words, the selection logic is based on business fit and outreach value, not on a single industry label.
