The DT CultureTech Business Analytics assignment asks candidates to simulate a **real sales research function**: identify companies that DT should target as clients, score them using a structured framework, document the research process, and produce a GitHub-ready professional deliverable.

DT CultureTech's business model depends on acquiring B2B clients (product startups) and deploying their SD LMS within those companies. A Business Analytics intern's job is to build and qualify this pipeline. The assignment tests whether the candidate:

### Common Mistakes Candidates Make

1. **Choosing generic companies** — listing ITC, Infosys, or Wipro as targets shows no understanding of DT's ICP (seed-to-Series D product startups)
2. **Uniform scores** — giving every company 4/5 on every criterion means no discrimination; it signals the candidate didn't actually research
3. **Hidden assumptions** — treating self-reported figures as audited facts without flagging uncertainty
4. **Ignoring the "why"** — listing companies without explaining why they fit DT specifically
5. **Generic personalization hooks** — "I noticed your company is growing" is meaningless; hooks must be specific and recent
6. **No rejection criteria** — a real pipeline filters companies OUT; a list with zero rejected companies is suspicious
7. **AI-copy output** — overly polished, structurally identical paragraphs for every company signal pure AI output

## Scoring Framework

### DT CultureTech ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

DT CultureTech's SD LMS is relevant to companies that:
- Have an active product team (10–500 employees)
- Are in innovation-adjacent sectors (EdTech, FinTech, HRTech, SaaS)
- Are founder-led or have innovation-conscious leadership
- Have secured funding (Seed to Series D) — signals budget availability
- Are growing fast enough to need structured team development

### Six-Criteria Scoring System (Each 1–5, Max 30)

| Criterion | What It Measures | Score 1 | Score 3 | Score 5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **C1: Innovation Culture Readiness** | Does the company invest in team dev and product thinking? | No evidence | Some signs (blog posts, L&D mentions) | Documented culture (public talks, R&D team, job descriptions) |
| **C2: Team Size Fit** | Is the team large enough to justify DT's platform? | <10 or >1000 | 10–50 | 51–500 (DT's sweet spot) |
| **C3: Decision Maker LinkedIn Access** | Can DT reach the founder/CTO via LinkedIn? | No profile | Profile exists, low activity | Active poster, large network, publicly reachable |
| **C4: Budget Readiness** | Can the company afford DT's platform? | Pre-seed, no funding | Seed or bootstrapped (limited budget) | Series A+ or profitable — budget confirmed |
| **C5: Personalization Hook Strength** | Is there a specific recent trigger for outreach? | No recent news | General growth news | Specific, recent, unique trigger (funding, launch, award) |
| **C6: Domain Alignment** | How closely does DT's LMS match this company's sector? | Unrelated | Adjacent (FinTech, HRTech) | Direct (EdTech, Bootcamp, Learning Platform) |

### Verdict Bands

| Verdict | Score Range | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **HOT** | 22–30 | Immediate outreach priority — strong ICP fit |
| **WARM** | 14–21 | Monitor and reach out when a trigger event occurs |
| **COLD** | ≤13 | Remove from pipeline — poor ICP fit |

## Key Insights from the Research

### Insight 1: Founders Who Show Up Publicly Convert Better

The strongest targets are the ones where the founder or leadership team is already visible on LinkedIn and public channels. In practice, that means the first message can be specific and grounded instead of generic. These companies are easier to approach because there is already a public trail of how they think about growth, learning, and team capability.

**Implication:** Start outreach with founders and visible leaders rather than going through only HR or generic company inboxes.

### Insight 2: EdTech and Product-Led Learning Companies Are the Easiest Fit

EdTech companies such as Scaler, Classplus, Newton School, Masai, Teachmint, Leverage Edu, Uolo, and Sunstone sit closest to DT’s offer. The reason is simple: DT is selling a structured learning product, so the value is easier to explain to companies that already care about learning outcomes and product-led growth.

**Implication:** EdTech should stay the primary vertical, with fintech and HRTech used as secondary segments.

### Insight 3: Existing DT Clients Still Matter for Expansion

Growpital, Kringle.ai, and IPx are already linked to DT. That does not make them the biggest revenue targets, but it does make them useful for account expansion. Existing clients are often easier to deepen than cold accounts, especially when the product team or leadership team already knows the brand.

**Implication:** Keep a separate follow-up list for existing clients so they do not get lost in new outreach.

### Insight 4: Hyderabad-Based Companies Are Easier to Work With

Hyderabad companies such as Darwinbox, Keka HR, Zaggle, and some of the EdTech names are easier to justify in an outreach list because the local connection reduces friction. It also helps with credibility when the company is already hiring, growing, or actively building a team.

**Implication:** Local companies deserve a slightly higher priority because the outreach angle is simpler.

### Insight 5: Mid-Sized Teams Are the Sweet Spot

The best acquisition band is usually a company that is big enough to budget for learning and team development, but not so large that procurement becomes slow. In this list, the 51–200 and 201–500 employee bands are the most practical.

**Implication:** Mid-sized companies should be prioritized before very small firms or large enterprise-style teams.
